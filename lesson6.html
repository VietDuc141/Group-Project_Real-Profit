Fibonacci Retracement and Extension

+ 1. Definition.

* DEFINITION:

- The Fibonacci indicator was first introduced to the West in the 13th century by Leonardo Pisano Bogollo, an Italian mathematician.

- This sequence exhibits mathematical properties and ratios found in nature, such as in architecture and biology. Because Fibonacci appears universally throughout the universe, it is also present in financial markets.

- This is one of the reasons traders use the Fibonacci sequence in stocks, Forex, and Crypto to identify reversal points, set profit targets, and execute Fibonacci-based trading strategies.

* There are two main Fibonacci tools that traders commonly use in trading:

  • Purpose: To identify retracement levels of a trend. Based on personal experience, the key levels to focus on are 0.5 and 0.168.

  • Purpose: To find profit-taking points. The key levels to pay attention to are 0.5, 1, and 1.618.

+ 2. HOW TO DRAW FIBONACCI RETRACEMENT ?

  • For an Uptrend:

    - After the completion of an upward wave, a downward wave (correction wave) begins. Use the Fibonacci Retracement tool to draw from the Bottom of the wave to the Top of the previous upward wave.

  • For a Downtrend:

    - Reverse the process. Draw the Fibonacci Retracement from the Top of the wave to the Bottom of the previous downward wave.

* UPWARD TREND: To draw Fibonacci Retracement in an upward trend, connect three points:

  • Point 1: The bottom of the upward wave (start of the wave).

  • Point 2: The top of the upward wave (end of the wave).

  • Point 3: The bottom of the retracement wave (start of the correction).

* DOWNWARD TREND: For a downward trend, follow the opposite approach:

  • Point 1: The top of the downward wave (start of the wave).

  • Point 2: The bottom of the downward wave (end of the wave).

  • Point 3: The top of the retracement wave (start of the correction).
