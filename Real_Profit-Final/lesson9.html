Lesson contents

3 parts Expand All

+ 1. What is money management ?

- Money management involves managing your funds to protect your capital, minimize risks, and ensure sustainable profits over the long term.

+ 2. Example.

  • You deposit $1,000, making your total account balance $1,000.

  • - For a single trade:

    • You use $50 as margin.
    • You set a Stop Loss (SL) of -50% of $50, meaning a loss of $25 if the trade goes against you.
    • You set a minimum Take Profit (TP) of +50% of $50, meaning a profit of $25 if the trade succeeds.
    • Alternatively, you can extend profits (let them run) to 100% or even 1000% of the trade margin (+$50 or +$500).

  • In this scenario, if the trade loses, your maximum loss is $25. However, your profit potential is $25, $50, or $500, depending on your strategy.

+ 3. Money Management Strategy.
  • Treat your account balance as 100%.
  • For each trade, allocate 5% of your account balance.
  • Set your Stop Loss (SL) to a maximum loss of -50% of the allocated amount.
  • Set your Take Profit (TP) to a minimum gain of +50% of the allocated amount.
  • You can let profits run freely to 100% - 1000% of the trade margin if the trend favors you.
