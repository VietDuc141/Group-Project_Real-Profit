+ Case 1: Formation of a Double Bottom

* Formation of Bottoms :

  • The price creates a bottom and enters a correction zone to form a new peak (g) that is lower than the previous peak.
  • Following the peak, the price declines again, forming a new bottom that is lower than the previous bottom in terms of the lowest price
  • However, the closing price of bottom is higher than the lowest price of bottom, signaling potential support.

* Double Bottom Pattern as a Reversal Signal:

  • This price action forms a double bottom pattern, which indicates that a trend reversal from bearish to bullish has likely completed.
+ Case 2: Breakout and Confirmation.

* Price Breaks the Peak:

  • Wait for the price to break through the peak and form a new peak that confirms the bullish trend.

* Correction to Bottom:

  • After forming the new peak, the price corrects downward to create a bottom.

* Entry Point (Long Position):

  • Place a Long entry at bottom, assuming the bullish trend will continue after this correction.
+ 3. Key Notes

  • Confirmation of Trend Reversal:

    • The double bottom pattern combined with a breakout of the intermediate peak provides strong confirmation of the reversal.

  • Risk Management:

    • Place a stop loss below bottom or bottom, depending on the structure and volatility.

  • Take Profit Strategy:

    • Target levels can be set at the previous resistance zone or based on Fibonacci extensions (e.g., 127.2% or 161.8%).

  • Time Frame Relevance:

    • Apply this strategy on your main trading time frame for better context and reliability.
+ 4. How to Determine the Reversal Trend

* Key to Success.

  • When is a Downward Trend (after an Upward Trend) Broken?

    - A downward trend formed after an upward trend is broken when:

    • The price breaks above the highest peak of the downward trend.
    • The closing price remains consistently higher than the key resistance level that previously acted as a cap for the downtrend.

  • When is an Upward Trend (after a Downward Trend) Broken?

    - An upward trend formed after a downward trend is broken when:

    • The price breaks below the lowest trough of the upward trend.
    • The closing price remains consistently lower than the key support level that previously acted as a base for the uptrend.

* Concept of Reversal Trend.

- A reversal trend occurs when the market transitions from one trend to the opposite trend.

- Example:

  • Upward Trend → Reverses to Downward Trend:
    • The upward trend is broken first, followed by the formation of a downward trend.

* Conditions for a Reversal Trend:

  • Necessary Condition (Breaking the Current Trend):

    - The current trend must be broken:

    • For an upward trend: Price breaks below the critical trough.
    • For a downward trend: Price breaks above the critical peak.

  • Sufficient Condition (Formation of Opposite Trend):

    - A new, opposite trend must form:

    • For a downward trend: A series of lower highs and lower lows.
    • For an upward trend: A series of higher highs and higher lows.

* Key Principle:

Any condition occurring first is acceptable, but for a trend reversal to be confirmed,both conditions must occur:

  • The current trend must be broken.
  • A new opposite trend must be established.
