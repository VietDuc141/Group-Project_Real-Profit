+ 1. What is Resistance and Support ?

* Support:

- Support is the price range where buying demand is strong enough to prevent further price declines.

  • When the price decreases to the support zone, buying demand increases significantly while selling demand decreases.
  • This prevents the price from dropping below this level and often causes the price to bounce back upward.

* Resistance:

Resistance is the price range where selling demand is strong enough to prevent further price increases.

  • When the price increases and reaches the resistance zone, the price tends to reverse and decline.
  • At this level, selling demand rises significantly, and buying demand weakens.

* Key Note:Support and resistance are zones, not just specific price points.

+ 2. How to Identify Support and Resistance ?

* Previous Lows and Highs: Past peaks and troughs serve as potential support and resistance levels.

* Market Behavior Explained:

  • Support Behavior:

    • When you buy a piece of land and its price decreases, your natural reaction is to hold, not sell.
    • If the price later drops to a previous low, it becomes attractive to new buyers who missed earlier opportunities, encouraging them to buy.
    • Sellers, meanwhile, are less motivated to sell at such low levels, reinforcing the support zone.

  • Resistance Behavior:

    • When the price rises to a previous peak (e.g., the price at which you initially bought the land), it motivates sellers to liquidate their holdings.
    • Buyers who entered at lower levels tend to take profits at this old peak since it represents a significant level in the market.
    • Prices above this peak are considered expensive, discouraging new buyers and causing the market to lose momentum.
+ 3. Notes on Support and Resistance.

* Role Reversal:

  • When the price breaks through a resistance level and forms a new peak, that resistance becomes a new support.
  • When the price breaks through a support level and forms a new low, that support becomes a new resistance.

* Strength of Support and Resistance:

  • The more frequently the price touches a support or resistance zone without breaking it, the stronger that zone becomes.

* Formation of New Levels:

  • When a support or resistance zone is broken, the price tends to move and establish a new support or resistance zone (creating a new low or a new peak).
